# 1. Executive Summary

Hawks Ridge is a 357-acre master-planned wellness community proposed for South Fulton, Georgia — one of the Atlanta metro's fastest-growing and most underserved corridors for premium real estate development.

The project combines for-sale residential housing (single-family homes in three tiers, cottages in two tiers, Beaux-Arts townhomes, 55+ age-qualified condos, and conventional condos for traditional buyers), an 80,000 SF premium athletic club, a full-spectrum wellness center, a curated mixed-use retail village, and permanently preserved green space — all within 15–20 minutes of downtown Atlanta.

Hawks Ridge is designed to fill a clear market gap: premium wellness-oriented community living at attainable price points, built for and by the New Black Middle Class in the fastest-growing metro in America.

# 2. Vision & Concept

Hawks Ridge draws inspiration from Serenbe but corrects its most significant limitations: distance from Atlanta, price exclusivity, and a demographics gap that leaves the region's largest growing buyer pool underserved.

## 2.1 The Four Pillars

**LIVE — For-Sale Residential**

A diverse range of ownership-oriented housing products designed for aspirational buyers: three tiers of detached single-family homes, stacked townhomes, and 55+ age-qualified condos — all within a walkable, nature-first village environment.

**MOVE — The Ridge Club**

An 80,000 SF premium athletic facility anchoring the community. Open to both residents and the broader South Fulton market, with full indoor facilities, outdoor courts, a 1.5-mile outdoor fitness trail, and direct connection to the 4.5-mile community trail network.

**HEAL — The Healing House**

A 22,000 SF full-spectrum wellness sanctuary offering nine distinct modality categories — float therapy, halotherapy, head spa, infrared sauna, herbal foot spa, sound healing, oxygen bar, facial studio, and chiropractic — with no comparable within 20 miles of South Fulton.

**BELONG — The Village**

A 65,000 SF curated mixed-use retail village featuring local food and beverage, wellness retail, professional services, community anchors, and boutique retail — with 40% of leasable area reserved for minority-owned and local businesses.

## 2.2 Design Philosophy

-   Minimum 18–27% of total land permanently preserved as green space, deed-restricted in perpetuity — final percentage determined by yield validation

-   Every home within 400 feet of a park, greenway, or trail

-   No cul-de-sacs — all streets connect to the community trail network

-   4.5 miles of connected walking and cycling trails linking all districts

-   French and Spanish inspired architecture with Georgia cottage character

-   Native Georgia plantings exclusively in all public landscapes

-   Dark-sky compliant lighting throughout the community

-   Carbon-neutral construction standards for all commercial buildings

-   Comprehensive EV charging infrastructure: 100% EV-ready homes + 22+ community charging stations with Community Trust revenue integration

South Fulton median home values have risen approximately 38% since 2019. The Langston Hughes district accounts for 73% of all new SFD closings in South Fulton over the last 12 months. Despite this growth, there is zero premium wellness-oriented master-planned community product serving this corridor.

The target demographic — Black professional households earning $100K–$250K annually — represents one of the fastest-growing buyer segments in the Atlanta metro, with significant pent-up demand for quality, community-oriented homeownership environments that reflect their values and aspirations.

## 5.4 Townhomes — 650 Units

Hawks Ridge townhomes are French Beaux-Arts row homes — the most architecturally distinctive residential product in the community. Modeled on the Parisian hotel particulier tradition, each unit presents a full limestone facade, ornate wrought iron balconies, and a private rooftop terrace. This is not a conventional townhome — it is a European-caliber row home at an attainable South Fulton price point. The program is sized at 650 units to maintain premium streetscape quality, manageable parking loads, and a genuine sense of place. Reducing from an earlier 840-unit concept improves absorption velocity, reduces political risk, and strengthens the premium positioning of each unit.

# 10. Safety & Security Program

Safety is a foundational design principle at Hawks Ridge — not an afterthought. The full safety program is built into the master plan from the ground up, integrating physical design, technology infrastructure, staffing, and community programming.

## 10.1 Entry & Access Control

-   Gated community — staffed main entry 24/7

-   Secondary gated entry for residents only — key fob and license plate recognition

-   Visitor management system — pre-registration via community app

-   Designated delivery and service vehicle access lane

-   Branded guard house at main entry monument

-   Boom barriers with backup power at all entry points

## 10.2 Surveillance Infrastructure

-   HD camera network covering all entry points, roads, parking areas, trails, and commercial zones

-   License plate recognition at all vehicle entry points

-   Camera coverage at every trail entry and park access point

-   24/7 remote monitoring via professional security operations center

-   30-day cloud video retention

-   Real-time alerts to residents via community app

# 17. Design Standards & Architectural Review

## 17.1 Design Philosophy

Every home at Hawks Ridge contributes to a unified streetscape. ## 17.2 Approved Exterior Materials

-   Brick: red, charcoal, or cream — running bond or Flemish bond only

-   Stucco: smooth or light sand finish — white, cream, sage, or warm gray only

-   Limestone: natural or cast — accent use on water tables, sills, and surrounds

-   Wood: painted only — no raw or stained wood on primary facades

**Shared Parking District Strategy**

The Ridge Club, Healing House, and Village lots are designed as a connected parking district — not three isolated lots. Internal circulation connects all three facilities so that users move freely between lots without re-entering the street. This shared-use approach produces an effective combined capacity of 940 spaces that peaks at different times for different uses, reducing the total land footprint required.

-   Ridge Club peak: Saturday 7–11am — high utilization

-   Village/farmers market peak: Saturday 9am–1pm — overlaps Ridge Club, shared district absorbs

-   Healing House peak: distributed throughout the day — lower simultaneous load than Ridge Club

-   Evening events (amphitheater, Village dining): Ridge Club lot largely available — district absorbs event overflow

-   Grass overflow lot: 2-acre reserved overflow area adjacent to the district activates only for concert events and major programming — unpaved, low cost, landscape-recoverable

**Parking Acreage Implication**

Correcting the parking program from 410 total spaces to 940 total spaces adds approximately 12–15 acres of parking surface to the commercial zone footprint. This is absorbed within the existing commercial acreage allocations — the Ridge Club, Healing House, and Village footprints were already generous enough to accommodate this. No additional land reallocation is required.

# 20. Brand & Identity

## 20.1 The Name — Hawks Ridge

The name Hawks Ridge draws from two sources rooted in the land and the legacy of South Fulton.

Hawks — the red-tailed hawk is native to the Georgia piedmont and a common sight along the creek corridors and tree canopy of the South Fulton landscape. The hawk represents vision, elevation, and freedom.

Ridge — the site sits on a natural topographic ridge that defines its character and creates the elevated views central to the community's design. The ridge is not a marketing invention — it is a physical feature of the land.
